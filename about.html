About

A laundry, run like a kitchen.

We started KC Launderette with a single belief: laundry deserves the same care a good restaurant gives its mise en place. What that means in practice is a small premises, well-chosen equipment, named team members, and a refusal to take shortcuts that show up months later in someone's wardrobe.

Why we started

KC Launderette began in 2024 above a small bookshop in Avondale, after one of the founders watched a favourite linen suit come back from a Harare dry cleaner crumpled, smelling of solvent, and missing two buttons. The conversation that followed was: there must be a better way to run this kind of business — one that takes garments seriously without becoming pretentious, and that puts the customer in direct contact with the people doing the work.

A launderette should feel like the inside of a well-run kitchen: calm, organised, and quietly excellent.

From day one we set three rules. We would never use stock photography on our website (we use photographs of our actual machines and the actual people who run them). We would never bury prices behind a quote form (every rate is published, openly). And we would route every conversation through WhatsApp — the platform our customers already used — so that the relationship lived where it would actually be useful.

The premises

The shop occupies a 220-square-metre ground-floor space at 14 Sample Avenue. Half is the public-facing receiving counter and pickup window; the other half is the working laundry — sorting tables, eight commercial Miele washers, four large-capacity dryers, two Veit pressing stations, and a folding bench long enough for a queen-size duvet to lie flat.

We deliberately chose a smaller footprint than the volume would justify. It keeps the team close, reduces handling between stations, and means every bag of laundry stays under one roof from drop-off to dispatch. There is no satellite facility, no overflow partner, no third-party processor. What you send to us, we wash.

We don't use call-centre handles or generic "Customer Care" sign-offs — when you message us, the person who replies tells you who they are.

The equipment

We use Miele professional washers because they last. The cost-per-wash over their service life is meaningfully lower than the cheaper alternatives, and the wash quality on delicate fabrics is materially better. Drying is split between a low-temperature large-capacity tumble dryer for towels and bedding, and a finishing room with hanging racks for shirts and dresses. Pressing is done on Veit cabinet presses for high-volume items and by hand on tailor's irons for fabrics that need a lighter touch.

Sustainability commitments

We use biodegradable detergents from a local supplier. The premises has a water recovery system that captures rinse water and reuses it on the pre-soak cycle, which cuts our fresh-water draw by roughly 30%. Our delivery van runs on petrol today, but we're committed to switching to an electric replacement when one becomes available locally — currently planned for 2027. None of these claims are marketing copy: each one is checked in our quarterly reports, and we'll publish the numbers when there's enough history to be honest about.

What you can expect from us

Specific times. Honest answers. Real photographs of any item we're worried about. A reply, on average, within six minutes of you sending a message during business hours. We don't always get every order perfect — when we don't, we say so, and we make it right within 48 hours.
